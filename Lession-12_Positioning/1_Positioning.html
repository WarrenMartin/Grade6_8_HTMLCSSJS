Understanding CSS Positioning

Importance of Positioning

CSS positioning plays a crucial role in web layout design. It allows you to control the placement of elements within a web page. Proper positioning ensures that your content appears as intended and makes your website user-friendly and visually appealing.

Types of Positioning

There are several types of positioning in CSS. Here, we'll briefly explain two common types:

1. Static Positioning

Elements with position: static are positioned according to the normal document flow. They are not affected by top, bottom, left, or right properties.

Static Position

2. Fixed Positioning

Elements with position: fixed are positioned relative to the viewport, and they stay in the same place even if you scroll the page. The top and right properties are used to specify their position.

Fixed Position
